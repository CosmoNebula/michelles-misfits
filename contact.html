Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about our rescue.

Our adoption process typically takes 3-7 days. It includes an application review, a brief phone or video interview, and a home environment check. We want to make sure every cat finds the perfect match, so we take the time to do it right.

Every adopted cat comes spayed/neutered, fully vaccinated (FVRCP + rabies), microchipped, FIV/FeLV tested, and dewormed. You'll also receive a starter kit with food, a toy, and veterinary records.

Yes, we do our best to help cats whose owners can no longer care for them. Due to limited foster space, we ask that you reach out to us first so we can assess availability. Please use the contact form above and select "Surrender Request" as your reason.

Contact us right away! If the cat is safe where it is, leave food and water nearby. If the cat appears injured or in danger, please take it to the nearest emergency vet. We can coordinate intake once we've assessed our foster capacity.

Absolutely! Many of our fosters have resident pets. We'll work with you to find a foster cat that matches your household. Some cats do great with other animals, while others may need a separate space. We'll help you find the right fit.
